Welcome to the World Museum's Asset Digitisation Solution.

By Georgia, Kyan, Kemal, Rhett, Bailey and Jean-Pierre.

From preserving and understanding history, fostering lifelong learning, or embracing cultural experiences; museums are crucial in ensuring history lives on forever. And as the state of the world is ever evolving, and so is technology; the digitisation of museum artefacts is more important than ever before.

We were given the task to digitise over 25 million scientific specimens and cultural artefacts located in the World Museum. Digitising their collection not only allows further collaboration in scientific research, but connects cultural objects back to their communities. There are 8 specimen categories, 8 business requirements to meet, and 5 years to complete the digital database.

Our solution covers the 8 business requirements, and topics 1, 3, and 4.

Throughout this website, we will discuss why our solution is the most beneficial, cost-effective and easiest to implement for the museum. Navigate to the Database page to learn about the chosen database software and online integration idea, the Imaging page to find out about the object digitalisation techniques, or the Tracking page where we will discuss how we plan to keep track of all the objects. We have also thoroughly planned out the timeline and budget constraints for the project, which you can read about on the Timeline page, as well as our extensive list of credible websites and papers we have referenced on the References page.
